# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Delivery Roles

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify requirements, discuss trade-offs, provide technical estimates
- **With Project Managers**: Report progress, flag blockers, participate in planning
- **With QA/Testing**: Collaborate on acceptance criteria, support testing efforts
- **With UX/UI Designer**: Implement designs, provide technical feasibility feedback
- **With DevOps Engineer**: Coordinate deployments, resolve infrastructure issues

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Align on priorities, discuss dependencies, share roadmap context
- **With Developers**: Discuss feasibility, clarify requirements, review implementation
- **With Business Analysts**: Validate requirements and scope definitions
- **With UX/UI Designer**: Define user experience goals, review design directions
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Incorporate user feedback into prioritization

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Coordinate on priorities and roadmap sequencing
- **With Developers**: Track progress, identify blockers, facilitate planning sessions
- **With Business Analysts**: Incorporate requirements into project timeline
- **With QA/Testing**: Coordinate testing phases and release readiness
- **With DevOps Engineer**: Align on deployment windows and infrastructure readiness

---

## Quality Assurance / Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals validate product quality, ensure acceptance criteria are met, and identify defects before release. They work closely with developers, product managers, and business stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Define and execute test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Perform manual and automated testing across feature sets
- Identify, document, and track defects with clear reproduction steps
- Collaborate on test strategy and coverage planning
- Validate release readiness and conduct smoke tests

### Goals
- Catch defects early and reduce production issues
- Maintain high test coverage and observability
- Ensure products meet user expectations and regulatory requirements

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and acceptance criteria reviews
- Defect reports and quality dashboards
- Test phase coordination during release cycles

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and user scenarios
- **With Developers**: Report defects, coordinate on reproduction and fixes
- **With Project Managers**: Track testing progress against milestones
- **With Business Analysts**: Validate requirement completeness before testing
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Incorporate user-reported issues into test plans

## Extended Team Roles

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers ensure that products are user-centric, usable, and visually aligned with brand standards. They bridge the gap between user needs and technical implementation through research, design, and iteration.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Collaborate with Product Managers to refine user experience goals
- Work with Developers to ensure designs are technically feasible
- Incorporate feedback from user testing and stakeholder reviews
- Maintain design systems and component libraries for consistency

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure brand consistency across products
- Support usability testing and continuous iteration

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Prototypes and mockups shared with stakeholders
- Accessibility and usability guidance during standups
- Design system documentation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user experience goals and success metrics
- **With Developers**: Review implementation against designs, discuss technical constraints
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate design timelines and dependencies
- **With QA/Testing**: Define usability acceptance criteria
- **With Business Analysts**: Validate design addresses user requirements

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and operational monitoring. They ensure systems are reliable, scalable, and secure, enabling teams to deliver and maintain products effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated testing and deployment
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and configuration management
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting for production systems
- Troubleshoot deployment failures and production incidents
- Advise development teams on infrastructure best practices and security

### Goals
- Enable rapid, reliable deployments with minimal manual overhead
- Maintain high system availability and performance
- Reduce deployment-related risks and incident response times
- Support scalability and cost efficiency

### Typical Communication
- Deployment runbooks and status reports
- Infrastructure guidance and technical design reviews
- Post-incident retrospectives and action items
- Operational metrics and dashboards

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Review code for deployment readiness, coordinate build pipeline improvements
- **With Project Managers**: Plan deployment windows and communicate release timelines
- **With QA/Testing**: Coordinate staging environments and smoke test execution
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Respond to operational issues, provide incident updates
- **With Product Managers**: Advise on scaling and reliability requirements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They gather, document, and clarify requirements, ensuring the team builds solutions that address real business needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements workshops
- Document business requirements and translate them into technical specifications
- Define acceptance criteria and validate scope completeness
- Identify dependencies and potential risks early
- Support change management and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and achievable
- Reduce scope creep and rework
- Improve stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Enable data-driven decision-making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specification sheets
- Acceptance criteria and definition of done reviews
- Status updates and scope change notifications
- Stakeholder briefings and discovery notes

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Refine product requirements and acceptance criteria
- **With Project Managers**: Identify scope, dependencies, and timeline impacts
- **With Developers**: Clarify technical feasibility and provide requirement details
- **With QA/Testing**: Define test scenarios aligned with requirements
- **With Business Stakeholders**: Gather requirements and validate alignment

---

## Support / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success teams are the voice of the customer. They gather user feedback, manage issue escalation, and ensure customer satisfaction and adoption.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and organize user feedback and feature requests
- Manage bug triage and escalation to development teams
- Communicate product updates and changes to customers
- Track customer satisfaction metrics and trends
- Support post-release adoption and provide user enablement

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Identify high-impact issues and features early
- Enable data-driven product decisions through user insights
- Reduce support burden through proactive communication

### Typical Communication
- Customer issue tracking and escalation reports
- Feature request summaries and prioritization input
- Product update announcements and user guidance
- Customer feedback and NPS metrics

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Share customer insights and prioritize feature requests
- **With Developers**: Report bugs and provide customer context for triage
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate release timelines and changes to customers
- **With QA/Testing**: Validate fixes for reported issues
- **With Business Analysts**: Provide user scenarios and real-world usage patterns
